Biography

The Architect of Modern India

Born on December 28, 1937, in Bombay, Ratan Naval Tata was destined to redefine corporate leadership and social responsibility globally.

He studied architecture and structural engineering at Cornell University, returning to India in 1962 to join the Tata Group. Far from a privileged entry, Ratan Tata started on the shop floor of Tata Steel in Jamshedpur, working alongside laborers, shoveling limestone, and learning the business from the roots.

When he succeeded J.R.D. Tata as the chairman of Tata Sons in 1991, India was entering economic liberalization. Over the next two decades, his visionary decisions propelled the Tata Group into a global powerhouse, all while holding high the principles of trust, ethics, and humanitarianism.

Launched the Tata Nano, the world's most affordable car. Inspired by a rainy day sight of a family of four balancing on a scooter, he fulfilled his promise of a safe, budget car for middle-class families.
